About Me

Hi, I’m a Critical Infrastructure Protection (CIP) specialist based in Portland, Oregon. I work at Portland General Electric, where I help keep the power grid secure by making sure all desktop computer assets in our energy management and physical security systems meet rigorous CIP security standards. I bring a strong background in information technology and electrical engineering to my work, with years of hands-on experience in the utilities industry. Whether it’s preparing for audits or keeping critical systems compliant, I take pride in getting the details right and staying ahead of what’s needed to keep infrastructure safe and reliable.

Experience

I've been deeply involved in CIP Compliance for more than a decade, with a proven track record of supporting large-scale energy systems through ongoing changes in regulations and technology.

At Portland General Electric, I specialize in managing and securing CIP-protected workstations—ensuring they meet all compliance controls under the North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC) standards. From updates and configurations to access controls and audit readiness, I handle the full lifecycle of these critical assets.

I've successfully completed three year-end certification exercises and participated in four Western Electricity Coordinating Council (WECC) audits—in 2014, 2017, 2020, and 2023—each one strengthening my knowledge and reinforcing the importance of proactive compliance.
